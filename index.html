Closing the Recycling Loop with AI & Salvaged Hardware

A scalable, low-cost smart station designed to turn complex mixed waste into high-value resources in high-traffic urban areas.

Power the Circular Economy

The Challenge: Valuable Waste is Going to Landfill

In developed countries, a massive portion of what ends up in landfills is actually recyclable. The main barriers are contamination, complex sorting rules, and a lack of convenient infrastructure.

30% to 75%

Of landfill waste is recyclable, depending on the region.

We aspire to the high standards of zero-waste towns like Kamikatsu, Japan (45 sorting categories), but we need a system that doesn't rely solely on intense citizen discipline. We need automation.

The Concept: A Corner-Shop-Sized Smart Station

We are building a small footprint station designed for high-foot-traffic areas like malls, large housing complexes, or supermarkets. It is designed to accept clean, dry waste that is often sorted improperly, such as e-waste, textiles, and complex plastics.

  • Convenient: Placed where people already go.
  • Scalable: Small footprint, easy to deploy.
  • Gamified: Users are rewarded with eco-points for correct sorting.

The Machine: Built from the Waste Stream

Our biggest competitive advantage is cost and circularity. We don't just sort waste; the machine itself is built from salvaged Commercial Off-The-Shelf (COTS) components.

How It Works: The AI Algorithm

We make complex sorting simple for the user through sophisticated backend technology.

1

Arrival

User approaches the station with dry waste. The station greets them via the salvaged display.

2

AI Scan

The user places an item on the sorting table. AI cameras identify the material and category.

3

Sorting Guide

The station indicates the correct bin (Glass, E-waste, Textile, etc.). Ambiguous items go to a holding area.

4

Reward

The user receives immediate feedback and 'green points'. Data is sent to the server.

If the AI is confused, the user can ask for help, connecting to AGI or human assistance.
